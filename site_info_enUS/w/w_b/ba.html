Project Design

The preliminary planning of Cambridge construction

      Han Stream drainage was originally an urban drainage. The original construction area was 106.03 square kilometers. Taichung Farmland Water Conservancy is characterized by the function of irrigation and drainage of the canal, which will be diverted to the area.

      Because of the population growth in the drainage area of the Han Stream and the high development and utilization of the land, the partial canal can only reproduce the flood peak discharges with a recurrence period of 2 to 5 years. The heavy rains often cause floods. Among them, the drainage channel from Jiyanqiao to Guoguang Bridge has been coped with. The environment in the water area is not good.

      It is imperative to combine the north side of Chung Hsing University and the south side of Kwong Kwong Park to prevent this area from been flooded and improve the overall visual landscape. Therefore, this City Council started the improvement of the river section landscape, the title of which was "Cambridge Project."

      The total length of the "Cambridge Project" is about 838 meters. The planned zoning includes the "ecological cleanup zone" in the upper reaches, the "waterfront recreation zone" in the middle reaches and the "detention flood protection zone" in the lower reaches. The "ecological cleanup zone".

( Image source: provided by the Third Rivers Bureau of the Department of Water Resources, Ministry of Economic Affairs)

       This project will create a space for informal banquets of green spaces along the banks of the Taiwan Strait and will enhance the beauty and vitality of the drainage channels in the region.

      When typhoons and heavy rains strike, there will be flood detention features. We will use the effect of runoff sharing and outflow control to create a quality water environment.

      The "Cambridge Project" fully communicates with the public at the planning and design stage. It also incorporates factors such as local expectations and local prosperity. With the joint efforts of the Water Resources Department, local water conservancy units, schools and community groups, Cambridge waterfront park has become a model water environment for other counties to learn from.
