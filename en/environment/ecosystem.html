1.Golden Shower Tree , Pudding Pipe Tree



Image source: Cassia fistula

Scientific name: Cassia fistula Linn

       Cassia fistula, known as the golden rain tree, canafistula and by other names, is a flowering plant in the family Fabaceae. The species is native to the Indian subcontinent and adjacent regions of Southeast Asia. It ranges from southern Pakistan eastward throughout India to Myanmar and Thailand and south to Sri Lanka. It is the state flower of Kerala in India and of immense importance amongst the Malayali population.

       It is a popular ornamental plant and is also used in herbal medicine. In ancient Tamil literature, it is called kondrai and is closely associated with the Mullai (forest) region of Sangam landscape. It is the national tree of Thailand, and its flower is Thailand's national flower.

2.Rose Wood , Burma Coast Padauk




Image source: Pterocarpus indicus Willd

Scientific name: Pterocarpus indicus Willd

      The Leaves of Rose Wood are odd pinnate, 15 to 35 cm long, and leaflets are alternate, 5 to 12, and occasionally 3. The shape of leaves is oval or oblong, leaf length 5 to 10 cm, leaf width 3 to 6 cm, apex sharp. The tip of the tail is sharp but the base of the leaf is blunt or round. The leaves are leathery thin with significant ribs, and the edge of the margin slightly wavy. The leaves are with fine veins and foliar is smooth.

       The tree grows bisexual flowers, right and left, raceme, pedicel and calyx with the same length. There are yellow flowers, shaped butterfly, sepals 5, inverted conical, blooming in April to May. The pods are oval, about 4 cm in size, and the central hypertrophy built-in one seed, like a poached egg, while the outer edge of the pod has a circle of flat wings. If cutting the wood of Indian red sandalwood, it will flow out purple juice. Hence it is named of India Rosewood.
