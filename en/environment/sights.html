228 Memorial Stone in Da-Li, Taichung

      On February 28, 1947 a massacre happened in Taiwan. After World War II, Japan returned Taiwan to China. That year the soldiers came to Taiwan to take over Taiwan. However, they took many original benefits from local people and were unjust to local people. So Taiwan people decided to protest violently.

      The soldiers led by Kuomintang-led Republic of China government killed thousands of civilians The massacre marked the beginning of the White Terror in which tens of thousands of other Taiwanese went missing, died or were imprisoned. The memorial stone is set here in memory of 228 incident.

Chung-Shin University

In 1919, Japanese built a “agriculture and forestry school” in Taipei. It was the origin of Chung-Shin University.

In 1928, transformed into Taipei Imperial University Affiliated Agriculture and Forestry specialized department

In 1943, transformed into “Chung-Shin University”

      Department of agriculture in Chung-Shin University has contributed a lot to Taiwan's agriculture for decades and also trained lots of professionals.
