       The grades of water quality (according to the law of water pollution prevention) :

First public water Refers to the water supply for public water after disinfection.
Two public water Refers to the coagulation, sedimentation, filtration, disinfection and other general methods of water purification for public water supply.
Three public water Refers to activated carbon adsorption, ion exchange, reverse osmosis and other special or highly available for public water supply.
First level of aquatic water Surface waters in the terrestrial land refers to sources of water that can be used for the cultivation of trout, sweetfish and sea bass. In the waters of the sea, it refers to sources of water for the cultivation of water-fed basil and seaweed.
Secondary aquaculture water In the terrestrial waters, refers to the waters available for the carp, grass carp and shellfish culture water; waters in the sea, referring to milkfish, mullet and asparagus cultivation of water sources of water.
First level of industrial water Refers to the source of water for the manufacture of water.
Secondary industrial water Refers to the water source for cooling water.
